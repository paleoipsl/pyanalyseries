Once you have defined a set of pointers to build your interpolation, you can apply it to any series of your choice.
<break time="1s"/>  

You can inspect the previously defined interpolation by pressing Ctrl D.
<break time="2s"/>  

Next, select the interpolation — referred to as the age model in paleoclimatology — and choose the series to be interpolated.
<break time="2s"/>  
Then select Apply Interpolation, depending on the method you want to use.
<break time="1s"/>  

A confirmation dialog appears.
<break time="1s"/>  

The interpolation is shown in red, and the series to which it will be applied are highlighted in green.
<break time="1s"/>  

The interpolated series appear below the original ones.
<break time="1s"/>  

Let’s inspect one interpolated series with Display Single or Ctrl D.
<break time="2s"/>  

You can see the new axis at the bottom, while the original axis remains at the top, now distorted.
Note that the zoom function applies simultaneously to both axes.
<break time="1s"/>  

The worksheet appears with an asterisk, indicating that it has been modified.
Press Ctrl S to save it.
<break time="1s"/>
