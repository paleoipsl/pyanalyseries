In PyAnalySeries, just as we had different methods to estimate the Power Spectral Density, we also have several approaches to apply high-pass or band-pass filtering.
<break time="1s"/>

Let’s return to our example and explore the filtering options.
<break time="1s"/>

We select the resampled PercAndes record, and choose: Process, Frequency Filter.
<break time="2s"/>

Different filtering methods can be tested, such as Butterworth, Lanczos, FIR for Finite Impulse Response, or Savitzky–Golay.
<break time="6s"/>

It is important to clearly understand the method being applied. The documentation is available for that purpose.
<break time="4s"/>

The workflow remains the same: we explore different parameter settings, clear the stack of intermediate tests when needed, and save once the result is satisfactory.
<break time="1s"/>

We know from the published study that precessional periodicities, around 19 and 23 thousand years, are significant in the PercAndes record from this core.
<break time="1s"/>

We now filter the record within this range of periodicities.
<break time="1s"/>

Here, we choose a Butterworth period band filter, keeping periodicities between 18 and 25 thousand years.
<break time="6s"/>

Save the filtered series.
<break time="2s"/>

You can now display both the original and the frequency-filtered series together.
<break time="1s"/>

Select both series, and press Control T to display them together.
<break time="6s"/>

We can now compare this filtered signal to insolation variations.
<break time="2s"/>
