Before going further,
let’s briefly review the sampling capabilities available in PyAnalySeries.
<break time="1s"/>  

The application allows you to redefine the sampling of your series
in a flexible and controlled way.
<break time="1s"/>  

You can resample a dataset either using a regular step,
or based on the X-values of another series.
<break time="1s"/>  

An integration option is also available,
allowing values to be averaged over intervals.
<break time="1s"/>

Several interpolation methods are available,
depending on the nature of your data
and the level of precision required.
<break time="1s"/>  

These tools help standardize your datasets,
make comparisons more robust,
and prepare the data for further analysis or visualization.
<break time="1s"/>  

Let’s now look at a concrete example.
<break time="1s"/>  

We now focus on the record called “PercAndes”,
which reflects the relative contribution of Andean versus lowland input
from Amazonian terrigenous sediments deposited at the core site.
<break time="1s"/>

The record exhibits uneven temporal resolution over time,
due to a reduced sedimentation rate in the lower part of the record.
<break time="1s"/>

To address this issue,
we resample the record using a regular step size.
<break time="1s"/>

Select the series,
then go to Process → Define Sampling.
<break time="2s"/>

Define the sampling parameters.
<break time="1s"/>

For example,
resample the record every 250 years
by entering 0.25 in the sampling step field.
<break time="1s"/>

The record now has a regular spacing of 0.25 kyr.
<break time="1s"/>

You can then save both the sampling configuration and the resampled series.
<break time="1s"/>
