PyAnalySeries provides a simple and efficient way to smooth time series data.
<break time="1s"/>

Filtering is applied in the X-domain,
using a centered moving average based on a convolution with a square window.
<break time="1s"/>

Each value is replaced by the average of its neighboring points.
<break time="1s"/>

This reduces high-frequency variability
while preserving the main trends of the signal.
<break time="1s"/>

Other X-domain filtering methods could be implemented if needed.
<break time="1s"/>

Let’s return to our practical example.
<break time="1s"/>

The “PercAndes” record remains slightly noisy, even after resampling at 0.25 thousand years.
<break time="1s"/>

We therefore apply a smoothing average to improve readability.
<break time="1s"/>

Select the record, then go to Process and choose “Define Smoothing Average”.
<break time="1s"/>

You can test different window sizes depending on the level of smoothing required.
<break time="1s"/>

Here, we use a 5-point running average.
Given the 0.25 thousand years sampling, this corresponds to a smoothing window of 1 thousand years.
<break time="1s"/>

Most of the high-frequency variability is effectively removed,
while preserving the main trends of the signal.
<break time="1s"/>

You can then save the filtered series and the filter
for later reuse on other series.
<break time="2s"/>

You can also hover over the item ID
to display its history.
<break time="3s"/>
